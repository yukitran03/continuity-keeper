# Saltglass — demo corpus

> An original short serial used to generate real canon on Walrus (no third-party IP).
> The point isn't the prose — it's that Continuity Keeper reads canon from these scenes,
> keeps a later scene consistent, and correctly **retires** canon when the story changes.

## Premise
The Grey Tide eats memory. In the half-drowned city of **Ravengard**, people keep what they
love in **saltglass** towers. **Memory-divers** descend into **the Fold** — a rift beyond the
city — to recover memories the Tide has stolen. But the Fold takes a price: **each dive costs
the diver one memory they can never get back.**

## Ch.1 — The first dive
Elara went down against her brother's warning. Kane, harbor-warden, shouted from the pier as
she slipped past the last saltglass tower. She surfaced with a stranger's laughter in her hands
— and could no longer remember her mother's face. Her grey eyes held the loss; the saltglass
pendant at her throat, her mother's last memory, felt suddenly heavier.

## Ch.3 — The Sunblade at the harbor
When the Grey Tide climbed the harbor mouth, Kane raised the Sunblade — the relic that burns
even underwater — and held the line until dawn. Mother Vess, blind keeper of the archive, read
the survivors' memories by touch and filed them in the towers.

## Ch.7 — The last dive
Kane's name had been stolen by the Fold. Elara dove one final time to bring it back. She did
not surface. The sea returned only her pendant, which Kane wore from that day.

## Ch.8 — (draft the agent must catch)
> "Elara laughed and drew the Sunblade from its sheath…"

This draft **breaks canon two ways** — Elara died at the Fold in Ch.7, and the Sunblade is
Kane's, not hers. Continuity Keeper should stop and flag it instead of writing it.
